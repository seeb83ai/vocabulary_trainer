Understanding the Metrics

  • Ease Factor — An SM-2 value (1.3–2.5+) reflecting how easy a word is for you. Starts at 2.5; drops with wrong answers, rises with correct ones. Higher = easier = longer review intervals.
  • Milestones — How many words you've answered correctly at least 1, 3, 5, or 10 times. Shows your depth of practice.
  • Accuracy Buckets — Words grouped by your correct-answer rate. Aim to move words from red/yellow into the green zone.
  • Avg / Median / P95 — Average is the mean across all seen words. Median is the middle value (less affected by outliers). P95 is the 95th percentile — the value that 95% of your words fall at or below.
  • Hardest Words — Words with the lowest accuracy (min 3 attempts). These need the most attention.
  • Most Practiced — Words you've attempted the most. High practice + low accuracy = stubborn words.
